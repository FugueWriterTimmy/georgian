Georgian nouns are relatively simple and can be explained in three points:

1. The Three Noun Classes

2. Case Declension Endings

Georgian exhibits split ergativity, meaning depending on the screeve series, nouns take different cases. Class 4 verbs and all Series III verbs put the subject in the Dative and objects in the Nominative, Class 2 verbs keep the subject Nominative in Series I and II, while Class 1/3 verbs use a Nominative-Dative pattern in Series I that shifts to an Ergative-Nominative-Dative pattern in Series II.

3. Plural Formation & Syncope

Plurals insert -eb- before case endings. The final ი and ა are dropped (დედა → დედები).

The A/E Syncope Rule: If a word ends რ, ლ, მ, ნ, ზ, ც, or ყ, the internal syllable vowel ა or ე is squeezed out completely when adding -eb- or putting the noun in the Genitive, Instrumental, or Adverbial case if the resulting consonant cluster does not exceed two or create a geminated consonant pair or an illegal voice clash (-თდ-, -დთ-, etc.). If the vowel is ო, it turns to ვ unless the final (ending) vowel in the nominative is ო.
